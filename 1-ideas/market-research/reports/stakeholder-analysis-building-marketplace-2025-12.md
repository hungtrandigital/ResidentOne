# Stakeholder Analysis - Building Marketplace Platform

**Date:** 2025-12-12  
**Last Updated:** 2025-12-12  
**Research Type:** Stakeholder Needs Analysis  
**Geographic Focus:** Vietnam (HCMC, Hanoi)

## Executive Summary

This report analyzes the needs, pain points, and value propositions for all key stakeholders in a building marketplace platform. Understanding each stakeholder's perspective is critical for designing a platform that serves the entire building ecosystem.

## Stakeholder Overview

| Stakeholder | Role | Key Needs | Pain Points |
|------------|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Building Management Team** | Platform administrators, building operations | Efficient communication, resident engagement, operational support | Fragmented communication, manual processes |
| **Building BQT** | Decision makers, building governance (elected by residents) | Resident satisfaction, building value, community building | Limited resident engagement tools, BQT turnover every 3 years |
| **Residents** | General users, community members | Easy access to services, information, community connection | Hard to find information, fragmented groups |
| **Sellers (Housewives)** | Active sellers, community entrepreneurs | Easy selling, reach buyers, make income | Hard to reach buyers, no selling tools |
| **Buyers** | Purchasers, service seekers | Find items/services, trust sellers, easy transactions | Scams, hard to search, trust issues |
| **Information Sharers** | Community helpers, information providers | Share building info (hotlines, contacts) | No centralized place to share |

## 1. Building Management Team

### Role & Responsibilities
- Manage building operations and maintenance
- Handle resident communications
- Coordinate with service providers (technicians, cleaners, security)
- Maintain building facilities and services
- Respond to resident requests and complaints

### Current Pain Points
1. **Fragmented Communication:**
   - Multiple Facebook/Zalo groups to monitor
   - Hard to reach all residents with important announcements
   - No centralized communication channel

2. **Manual Processes:**
   - Manual coordination of service requests
   - No automated system for tracking resident needs
   - Time-consuming to manage multiple groups

3. **Limited Visibility:**
   - Can't see what residents are trading/selling
   - No insights into resident needs and preferences
   - Hard to identify building community trends

4. **Service Provider Coordination:**
   - Hard to share service provider contacts (technician, police, accountant, worker hotlines)
   - No centralized directory of building services
   - Residents constantly ask for same information

5. **BQT/BQL Turnover Problem (CRITICAL):**
   - **BQT (Ban Quản Trị) is elected every 3 years (some buildings 1-2 years, some continuously)**
   - **BQT turnover often leads to BQL (Ban Quản Lý) change**
   - **When BQT/BQL changes, the app changes too (depending on new management)**
   - **Resident information is not updated - takes 2-3 months to update each time BQT/BQL changes - very tiring**
   - **Data continuity is lost when BQT/BQL changes**
   - **Each new BQL has to rebuild resident database from scratch**
   - **Platform dependency on BQT/BQL creates instability**

6. **Inefficient Management Processes:**
   - **Notifications are tiring and annoying - current methods are inefficient**
   - **Opinion polls/surveys are tiring and annoying - no good system**
   - **Manual processes for resident engagement**
   - **No efficient way to gather resident feedback**
   - **Time-consuming to manage resident communications**

### Needs & Expectations
- **Centralized Communication:** One platform to reach all residents
- **Service Directory:** Easy way to share and maintain building service contacts (hotlines)
- **Resident Engagement:** Tools to engage residents and build community
- **Operational Efficiency:** Reduce manual work, automate processes
- **Community Building:** Support resident-to-resident interactions
- **Data Continuity:** Platform that survives BQT/BQL changes - resident data persists
- **Efficient Notifications:** Better notification system (not tiring/annoying)
- **Easy Polling/Surveys:** Efficient system for opinion polls and resident feedback
- **Resident Data Management:** Easy way to maintain and update resident information
- **Platform Independence:** Platform not dependent on BQL changes

### Value Proposition
- **Efficiency:** One platform instead of multiple groups
- **Better Communication:** Reach all residents with important announcements
- **Service Support:** Easy way to share service provider contacts
- **Community Management:** Tools to manage and engage building community
- **Reduced Workload:** Less time managing fragmented groups
- **Data Continuity:** Resident data persists through BQT/BQL changes - no need to rebuild database
- **Efficient Notifications:** Better notification system that's not tiring/annoying
- **Easy Polling:** Efficient system for opinion polls and resident feedback
- **Platform Stability:** Platform survives BQT elections and BQL changes - no need to switch apps
- **Time Savings:** No 2-3 month data update period when BQT/BQL changes
- **Election-Proof:** Platform survives 3-year BQT election cycle

### Platform Features Needed
1. **Announcement System:** Broadcast important building information (efficient, not tiring)
2. **Service Directory:** Maintain and share building service contacts (technician, police, accountant, worker hotlines)
3. **Resident Management:** View resident activity and engagement
4. **Moderation Tools:** Manage content and ensure platform safety
5. **Analytics:** Insights into resident needs and platform usage
6. **Data Continuity System:** Resident data persists through BQT/BQL changes
7. **Efficient Notification System:** Smart notifications that are not tiring/annoying
8. **Polling/Survey System:** Easy way to conduct opinion polls and gather resident feedback
9. **Resident Data Management:** Easy way to maintain and update resident information
10. **BQT/BQL Transition Tools:** Smooth handover when management changes
11. **Election Independence:** Platform survives BQT election cycles (every 3 years)

## 2. Building BQT (Ban Quản Trị - Board of Directors)

### Role & Responsibilities
- **Elected by residents** - represents building owners/residents
- Make strategic decisions for building
- **Supervise BQL (Ban Quản Lý)** - oversee management company
- **Manage maintenance fund**
- Ensure resident satisfaction
- Maintain building value and reputation
- Build strong building community
- **Sign contracts with BQL**

### Current Pain Points
1. **Limited Resident Engagement:**
   - Hard to gauge resident satisfaction
   - Limited tools to build community
   - No visibility into resident needs

2. **Building Value:**
   - Can't easily demonstrate building value to potential residents
   - Limited community features compared to newer buildings
   - Hard to showcase building amenities and services

3. **Communication Gaps:**
   - Important decisions not reaching all residents
   - No efficient feedback mechanism
   - Limited transparency in building operations

4. **BQT Turnover:**
   - **BQT is elected every 3 years (some 1-2 years, some continuously)**
   - **BQT turnover often leads to BQL change and app/platform change**
   - **Platform instability** - apps change with each BQT election
   - **Data loss** - resident data lost when management changes

### Needs & Expectations
- **Resident Satisfaction:** Tools to improve resident experience
- **Community Building:** Platform that strengthens building community
- **Building Value:** Features that add value to building and attract residents
- **Transparency:** Better communication with residents
- **Modern Image:** Platform that makes building appear modern and well-managed
- **Platform Stability:** Platform that survives BQT election cycles (every 3 years)
- **Data Continuity:** Resident data persists through BQT/BQL changes
- **Efficient Management:** Tools to efficiently manage building and residents

### Value Proposition
- **Enhanced Building Value:** Modern platform increases building appeal
- **Better Resident Satisfaction:** Improved communication and services
- **Community Strength:** Stronger resident community
- **Competitive Advantage:** Better than buildings without such platform
- **Decision Support:** Data and insights for better decision-making
- **Platform Stability:** Platform survives BQT election cycles - no disruption
- **Data Continuity:** Resident data persists through BQT/BQL changes
- **Election-Proof:** Platform not affected by 3-year BQT election cycle

### Platform Features Needed
1. **Community Metrics:** Dashboard showing resident engagement
2. **Feedback System:** Easy way to gather resident feedback
3. **Building Showcase:** Highlight building amenities and services
4. **Transparency Tools:** Share building decisions and updates
5. **Value Demonstration:** Show building value to current and potential residents
6. **Election Independence:** Platform survives BQT election cycles
7. **Data Continuity:** Resident data persists through BQT/BQL changes
8. **BQT Management Tools:** Tools to efficiently manage building and residents

## 3. Residents (General Users)

### Role & Responsibilities
- Live in building
- Participate in building community
- Buy/sell items and services
- Share information with neighbors
- Seek help and information

### Current Pain Points
1. **Information Finding:**
   - Hard to find building service contacts (technician, police, accountant, worker hotlines)
   - Important information scattered across multiple groups
   - No centralized information source

2. **Trust Issues:**
   - Can't verify if sellers are actual residents
   - Scams on Facebook/Zalo groups
   - Don't know who to trust

3. **Search Difficulty:**
   - Hard to find specific items/services
   - Multiple groups to search through
   - No unified search

4. **Community Connection:**
   - Limited ways to connect with neighbors
   - Hard to know what's happening in building
   - Missed opportunities for community engagement

### Needs & Expectations
- **Easy Information Access:** Quick access to building services and contacts
- **Trust & Safety:** Verified sellers and scam protection
- **Easy Search:** Find items/services easily
- **Community Connection:** Connect with neighbors
- **Convenience:** One platform for all building needs

### Value Proposition
- **Convenience:** One platform for all building needs
- **Trust:** Verified residents, scam protection
- **Information:** Easy access to building services and contacts
- **Community:** Better connection with neighbors
- **Safety:** Protected from scams

### Platform Features Needed
1. **Service Directory:** Easy access to building service contacts (hotlines)
2. **Verified Sellers:** Know sellers are actual residents
3. **Search & Discovery:** Easy to find items/services
4. **Community Features:** Connect with neighbors
5. **Information Hub:** Centralized building information

## 4. Sellers (Housewives)

### Role & Responsibilities
- Sell items and services to building residents
- Generate income from home
- Build customer base within building
- Manage inventory and listings
- Provide customer service

### Current Pain Points
1. **Limited Reach:**
   - Hard to reach potential buyers
   - Multiple groups to post in
   - No tools to proactively sell

2. **Passive Selling:**
   - Just post and wait
   - No way to promote listings
   - Can't target specific buyers

3. **Time Consuming:**
   - Post in multiple groups
   - Answer same questions repeatedly
   - Hard to manage listings

4. **Limited Tools:**
   - No analytics on what sells
   - Can't see buyer interest
   - No seller support features

5. **Food Sharing Challenge:**
   - Have big items from hometown (e.g., jackfruit)
   - Want to share/sell part of it
   - Hard to coordinate with multiple buyers
   - No tools for partial sharing/selling

### Needs & Expectations
- **Easy Selling:** Simple way to list and sell items
- **Reach Buyers:** Tools to reach more potential buyers
- **Proactive Sales:** Features to help sell more actively
- **Seller Support:** Tools and features to help sellers succeed
- **Community Selling:** Build relationships with buyers (neighbors)
- **Partial Sharing:** Tools to share/sell parts of items (e.g., jackfruit)

### Value Proposition
- **More Sales:** Reach more buyers, sell more items
- **Less Work:** Easier listing and management
- **Better Tools:** Seller features to help succeed
- **Community Trust:** Sell to verified neighbors
- **Income Generation:** Make money from home
- **Food Sharing:** Easy way to share/sell parts of items

### Platform Features Needed
1. **Seller Dashboard:** Manage listings and track sales
2. **Seller Groups:** Form groups to help each other sell
3. **Promotion Tools:** Features to promote listings
4. **Analytics:** See what sells, buyer interest
5. **Partial Sharing:** Tools to share/sell parts of items (e.g., jackfruit splitting)
6. **Customer Management:** Track buyers and build relationships
7. **Seller Support:** Resources and tools to help sellers succeed

### Use Case: Food Sharing (Jackfruit Example)
**Scenario:** Housewife receives big jackfruit from hometown, wants to share/sell parts of it

**Current Pain Points:**
- Hard to coordinate with multiple buyers
- No way to split item and track who gets what
- Payment coordination for partial shares
- Delivery/pickup coordination

**Platform Solution Needed:**
- **Partial Listing:** List item with option to buy parts
- **Group Buying:** Multiple buyers can join to split item
- **Coordination Tools:** Track who gets what part
- **Payment Splitting:** Easy payment for partial shares
- **Pickup Coordination:** Schedule pickup times for different buyers

## 5. Buyers

### Role & Responsibilities
- Purchase items and services from building residents
- Seek building services and information
- Participate in community transactions
- Make purchasing decisions based on trust

### Current Pain Points
1. **Trust Issues (CRITICAL):**
   - Can't verify if sellers are actual residents
   - Scams on Facebook/Zalo groups
   - **Trust is key behavior when making purchase decisions**
   - Don't know who to trust

2. **Search Difficulty:**
   - Hard to find specific items/services
   - Multiple groups to search
   - No unified search

3. **Information Access:**
   - Hard to find building service contacts
   - Important information scattered
   - No centralized information

4. **Transaction Friction:**
   - No integrated payment
   - Manual coordination
   - Hard to track purchases

### Needs & Expectations
- **Trust & Safety:** Verified sellers, scam protection
- **Easy Search:** Find items/services easily
- **Information Access:** Quick access to building services
- **Convenient Transactions:** Easy payment and coordination
- **Community Connection:** Buy from neighbors

### Value Proposition
- **Trust:** Verified sellers (residents), scam protection
- **Convenience:** Easy search and transactions
- **Safety:** Protected from scams
- **Community:** Buy from verified neighbors
- **Information:** Easy access to building services

### Platform Features Needed
1. **Verified Sellers:** Know sellers are actual residents
2. **Search & Filters:** Easy to find items/services
3. **Service Directory:** Access to building service contacts
4. **Payment Integration:** Easy and secure payment
5. **Reviews & Ratings:** See seller reputation
6. **Transaction Tracking:** Track purchases and deliveries

### Buyer Decision Behavior
**Key Insight:** **TRUST is the primary behavior when buyers make purchase decisions**

**Trust Factors:**
- Verified resident status
- Reviews and ratings from other residents
- Building community context
- Scam protection
- Neighbor-to-neighbor transactions

**Platform Must Address:**
- Verification system (critical)
- Reviews and ratings
- Scam protection
- Community trust building
- Transparent seller information

### Current Pain Points

### Needs & Expectations
- **Centralized Information:** One place to share and find building information
- **Easy Sharing:** Simple way to share contacts and information
- **Searchable Directory:** Easy to find specific information
- **Community Resource:** Help neighbors find what they need

### Value Proposition
- **Helpful:** Easy way to help neighbors
- **Efficient:** Reduce repetitive questions
- **Community Building:** Strengthen building community
- **Resource Hub:** Centralized building information

### Platform Features Needed
1. **Service Directory:** Maintain building service contacts (hotlines)
2. **Information Sharing:** Easy way to share information
3. **Searchable Database:** Find information quickly
4. **FAQ Section:** Common questions and answers
5. **Community Wiki:** Building information repository

## 7. Food Sharing Use Case (Special Scenario)

### Scenario
Residents receive large items from hometown (e.g., jackfruit, durian, large quantities of food) and want to:
- Share with neighbors
- Sell parts of it
- Coordinate with multiple buyers
- Split costs and delivery

### Current Pain Points
1. **Coordination Challenge:**
   - Hard to coordinate with multiple buyers
   - No way to split item and track shares
   - Payment coordination for partial shares

2. **Delivery/Pickup:**
   - Hard to schedule multiple pickups
   - No coordination tools
   - Manual communication

3. **Trust & Safety:**
   - Need to trust buyers
   - Payment security
   - Quality assurance

### Platform Solution Needed
1. **Partial Listing Feature:**
   - List item with option to buy parts
   - Set minimum share size
   - Track available shares

2. **Group Buying:**
   - Multiple buyers can join to split item
   - Automatic coordination
   - Track who gets what part

3. **Payment Splitting:**
   - Easy payment for partial shares
   - Automatic calculation
   - Secure transactions

4. **Pickup Coordination:**
   - Schedule pickup times
   - Notify buyers when ready
   - Track pickups

5. **Community Trust:**
   - Verified residents
   - Reviews for food quality
   - Safe transactions
