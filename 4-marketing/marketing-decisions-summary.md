## 1. Brand Foundation

### 1.1 Brand Name

- **Project Name**: ResidentOne
  - Rationale: Professional, scalable, works globally, links to "OneHub" product naming

- **Product Naming Strategy** (Priority Order):
  1. **[Tên Chung Cư]** - Simple, clean, professional
  2. **[Tên Chung Cư] OneHub** or **[Tên Chung Cư] Hub** - Comprehensive hub concept
  3. **Chợ [Tên Chung Cư]** - Easy to remember but too focused on "marketplace", loses management/announcement aspect
  - **Decision**: Use Option 1 or 2 depending on building preference
  - **Rationale**: Balance between marketplace and management features, professional, reflects "Resident-Owned Community Hub" positioning

### 1.2 Brand Personality

**Primary**: Trustworthy, Reliable
- Key differentiator: Trust & Safety
- Critical for user adoption

**Secondary**: Warm, Community-Focused
- Community platform nature
- Building relationships

**Tertiary**: Modern, Efficient
- Tech solution
- Better than current methods

### 1.3 Brand Positioning

**Primary Message Priority:**
1. **Trust & Safety** - Verified residents, scam protection, system-wide blocking
2. **Resident-Owned** - Survives BQT/BQL changes, data continuity
3. **Community-Focused** - Building community, not just transactions

**Positioning Statement:**
> "For building communities in Vietnam who need a reliable platform that survives management changes and enables safe commerce, our platform is a resident-owned community hub that provides essential support services and enables transparent, safe, efficient commerce."

## 3. Target Audience Strategy

### 3.1 Primary Target (First 10 Buildings)

**Priority: BQL/BQT (Building Management) from Trung cấp (Mid-range) Segment**
- **Segment:** Trung cấp (Mid-range) - 30-50M VND/m²
- **Rationale:** 
  - Best balance of volume and willingness to pay
  - Professional management (easier to work with)
  - High digital adoption (easier onboarding)
  - Quality-focused (aligns with our value proposition)
  - Good budget for platform subscription
  - Direct decision makers, can onboard building immediately
- **Approach:** High-touch, personal connections, direct sales

### 3.2 Secondary Target (Next 20-30 Buildings)

**Property Developers (Chủ Đầu Tư) + Bình dân (Affordable) Segment**
- **Segment:** Bình dân (Affordable) - 20-30M VND/m²
- **Rationale:** 
  - Large volume potential
  - Price-sensitive but value-conscious
  - Can use freemium model
  - Good for scale
  - Bulk acquisition, multiple buildings per developer
- **Approach:** Partnerships, revenue share model, freemium pricing

### 3.3 Tertiary Target (Scale Phase)

**BQL Companies (Công Ty Quản Lý Tòa Nhà) + Cao cấp (Premium) Segment**
- **Segment:** Cao cấp (Premium) - 50-100M+ VND/m²
- **Rationale:** 
  - High willingness to pay
  - Premium features and white-label
  - Lower volume but high value
  - Partnerships, scale quickly, white-label option
- **Approach:** Bulk licensing, revenue share, premium pricing, white-label

### 3.4 Building Segment Strategy

**Segment Priority (Based on Market Analysis):**
1. **Trung cấp (Mid-range) - PRIMARY TARGET**
   - Price: 30-50M VND/m²
   - Characteristics: Middle-income, professionals, quality-focused
   - Rationale: Best balance of volume and willingness to pay
   - Approach: Direct sales, professional messaging, quality-focused

2. **Bình dân (Affordable) - SECONDARY TARGET**
   - Price: 20-30M VND/m²
   - Characteristics: Budget-conscious, value-focused
   - Rationale: Large volume, good for scale
   - Approach: Freemium model, value messaging, partnerships

3. **Cao cấp (Premium) - TERTIARY TARGET**
   - Price: 50-100M+ VND/m²
   - Characteristics: High-income, executives, premium-focused
   - Rationale: High willingness to pay, premium features
   - Approach: Premium pricing, white-label, exclusive messaging

4. **Nhà ở Xã hội (Social Housing) - FUTURE**
   - Price: <20M VND/m²
   - Characteristics: Government-subsidized, low-income
   - Rationale: Large volume potential, but lower willingness to pay
   - Approach: Different model needed (government support?)

**See [Building Segment Analysis](1-ideas/1.1-market-research/reports/building-segment-analysis-behavior-differences-2025-12.md) for detailed analysis.**

### 3.5 Building Types Analysis (To Be Researched)

**Categories to Analyze:**
1. **Buildings with existing management apps**
   - Pain Point: App dependent on BQT/BQL, data loss
   - Approach: Emphasize "Resident-Owned" + "Data Continuity"
   - Decision Maker: BQL/BQT (may have resistance)

2. **Buildings without apps**
   - Pain Point: Manual processes, fragmented (Facebook/Zalo groups)
   - Approach: Emphasize "Comprehensive Hub" + "Efficiency"
   - Decision Maker: BQL/BQT (easier sell, no existing solution)

3. **Buildings with active FB marketplace groups**
   - Pain Point: Scams, hard to search, fragmented
   - Approach: Emphasize "Trust & Safety" + "Better Organization"
   - Decision Maker: FB group admin or residents (grassroots)

**Status**: Need market research after MVP to determine priority and approach for each type

- Referral Program
- Digital Marketing
- Optimization and conversion improvement

### 4.4 Community Ambassador Model

**Approach: Smart Community-Based Acquisition**

**Strategy:**
- Find 1 person in targeted building
- That person becomes community ambassador/point of contact
- If successful → commission/revenue share based on KPIs

**KPI Structure (Phased):**

**Phase 1: Onboarding**
- Reward when building subscribes/onboards
- Goal: Get building to use platform

**Phase 2: Launch & Activation**
- Active sellers & users (e.g., 50+ sellers, 200+ active users)
- Revenue/income metrics
- Goal: Solve biggest barrier - "no users" (app dies without users)

**Commission Structure:**
- To be determined: One-time onboarding reward + recurring commission based on performance
- Specific targets to be set after MVP testing

**Selection Criteria:**
- FB group admins (have influence)
- Active residents (community leaders)
- BQL/BQT members (official support)

## 6. Market Research Insights

### 6.1 Market Size (HCM Focus)

**Building Count:**
- **Total Buildings**: ~3,000 nationwide (primarily Hà Nội and HCM)
- **HCM**: Similar concentration to Hà Nội
- **Target Market**: 50-100 buildings Year 1

**Building Scale:**
- **Average Units**: 800-1,200 căn hộ/building
- **Average**: 1,000 căn hộ/building
- **Users per Building**: ~2,125 potential users/building

**Market Activity:**
- **H1 2024**: 14,400 apartment transactions (3x growth vs. 2023)
- **Supply**: 78,600 units expected H2 2025
- **Trend**: High-end supply increasing, buyers moving to adjacent areas

### 6.2 User Behavior Insights

**From MVP Experience:**
- 1 building: 60 sellers participated, 200-300 users registered
- Key learning: Operations need sufficient time/resources to maintain
- Event-driven approach successful for initial adoption

**Challenges:**
- Operations overhead (need dedicated resources)
- User activation (getting users to actually use platform)
- Content onboarding (need content to attract users)
